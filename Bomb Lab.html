Bomb Lab

Overview

Bomb Lab consists of six phases, each unique and more difficult to defuse progressively. There are 3 "free" attempts at exploding the bomb without being penalized, but after 3 attempts at exploding the bomb, the limit will be 20 explosions. Bomb Lab is a very complex project and difficult to understand at first but to give you an idea of what Bomb Lab actually is, I'll be going over phase one (the easiest of them all).

Phase One

This is Assembly Language. Phase 1 is a function checking for a specific input to be true and pop (return).

Looking at the first 2 lines (highlighted in pink), we're making space to push %rbx onto our stack and we're moving some value "%rdi" into %rbx. After reading the 3rd line we know that our answer is a character of length one given by the offset 0x1(%rdi).

Here we can see that our function is checking if our answer isn't a character of length one and isn't equal to what $0x6c and %rbx is being compared to, then the bomb will explode.

So we know our answer is $0x6c. Let's check what that is.

Since we know that our answer is a character and given that $0x6c is in Hexadecimal, we'll be using the Ascii Table to check it's value as a character. We can see that our Hexadecimal (highlighted in yellow) is a lowercase "l" as a character, therefore our answer is "l" and phase one is defused.
